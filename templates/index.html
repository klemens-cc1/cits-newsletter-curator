CITS Newsletter Curator — Guide
Overview

The CITS Newsletter Curator is a weekly article triage tool for the CITS energy security newsletter. Articles are ingested automatically from the RSS aggregator every Friday at 10 AM ET and organized by category. Reviewers mark articles for inclusion, flag uncertainties, or dismiss irrelevant content before exporting a structured reference file for newsletter writing.

Article statuses
Selected Included in exports. Applied to articles that will be referenced during writing. Maybe Pending decision. The "Selected + Maybe" export can be used to make a final call at the end of the week. Skip Not relevant. Article remains visible in the list but is dimmed and excluded from all exports.

Selecting an active status a second time returns the article to unreviewed.

Triage mode

Triage mode condenses the article list to title and source only, with status controls moved inline. A progress bar tracks reviewed articles against the total. Activated via the ⊟ Triage button in the header or the C keyboard shortcut.

Keyboard shortcuts
↑ / ↓ Move between articles S Selected M Maybe X Skip Enter Open article in new tab C Toggle triage mode ? Open / close this panel

Shortcuts are inactive when the search box or week selector is focused.

Export

Text — Selected produces a plain-text file organized by category, suitable for use as a writing reference. CSV — Selected includes all metadata fields and any cached article descriptions. Export options are available in the left sidebar.

Refresh

The ↻ Refresh button triggers a manual pull from the aggregator outside the automatic Friday schedule. A password is required. Ingested articles typically appear within two minutes. The refresh indicator in the top-right of the header shows the time of the last completed refresh and a log of recent refresh activity.

Article descriptions

The ↓ Pull description button fetches the publisher's meta description directly from the article page. No AI is involved — the description is the same text search engines and social platforms display for the article. Once fetched, descriptions are cached and displayed as a collapsible ▶ Description toggle on all subsequent loads.
